Decoding the AI Agent Zoo

A Typology of Minds

Simple Reflex Agents

The worker bees of the AI world. They react to stimuli with pre-programmed responses, devoid of memory or foresight. Efficient, but limited.

These agents operate based on condition-action rules: if this condition occurs, perform this action. They don't consider history or future consequences, making them suitable for simple, well-defined tasks in stable environments.

Model-Based Reflex Agents

A step up the evolutionary ladder. These agents maintain an internal model of the world, allowing them to make decisions based on predicted outcomes.

By maintaining a representation of the world's state, these agents can handle partially observable environments. They track the world's evolution and the impact of their actions, enabling more sophisticated decision-making.
